What is Artificial Intelligence?

Artificial Intelligence is the simulation of human cognitive processes by machines. Rather than relying on hardcoded rules (if-then statements), AI systems use probabilistic modeling to make decisions.

  • Narrow AI (ANI): Trained for one specific task (e.g., Facial Recognition).
  • General AI (AGI): Hypothetical AI with human-level cognitive flexibility.
  • Super AI (ASI): Surpassing human intelligence.

AI vs. ML vs. DL

Artificial Intelligence

The broad concept of machines being able to carry out tasks in a way that we consider "smart".

Machine Learning

An application of AI based on the idea that we should give machines access to data and let them learn for themselves.

Deep Learning

A subset of ML that uses multi-layered artificial neural networks to solve highly complex problems.

The Mathematical Foundation

As a Mathematics minor, I approach AI not as "magic," but as applied mathematics:

  • Linear Algebra: Data is represented as scalars, vectors, matrices, and tensors. Weights in a network are matrix multiplications.
  • Calculus: Used to calculate the gradients of the error function during backpropagation.
  • Probability & Statistics: Used to predict the likelihood of an output (e.g., Softmax classification).

The Physics Perspective

How does Physics relate to Machine Learning?

  • Energy-Based Models: Algorithms like Boltzmann Machines are directly inspired by statistical mechanics and thermodynamics.
  • Optimization & Entropy: Minimizing the "Loss Function" in AI is mathematically identical to finding the lowest energy state in a physical system.
  • Numerical Analysis: Solving differential equations (like the Euler method) shares principles with gradient descent step-sizes (learning rates).

How Machines "Learn"

Gradient Descent

Imagine standing on a mountain in the fog and trying to reach the bottom. You feel the slope with your feet and take a step downwards.

In AI, the "mountain" is the Error (Loss). The model iteratively adjusts its internal parameters (weights/biases) in the opposite direction of the gradient to minimize the error.

Data + Math Optimization = Learning

Artificial Neural Networks (ANN)

Inspired by biological brains, ANNs consist of interconnected nodes (neurons).

  • Input Layer: Receives the raw data (e.g., pixels of an image).
  • Hidden Layers: Applies mathematical transformations (activation functions like ReLU or Sigmoid) to extract features.
  • Output Layer: Delivers the final prediction.

Computer Vision: CNNs

Convolutional Neural Networks are specialized for processing grid-like data, such as images.

  • Filters/Kernels: Matrices that slide over the image to detect edges, textures, and shapes.
  • Pooling: Down-sampling the image to reduce computational load while retaining essential features.
  • Application: Medical imaging diagnostics, facial recognition, autonomous vehicles.

Time Series & Sequences: LSTMs

Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) and Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks possess an internal "memory."

Unlike standard networks, they feed outputs back into themselves, making them perfect for data where sequence matters.

  • Stock market price forecasting.
  • Weather pattern prediction.
  • Speech recognition.

Natural Language Processing (NLP)

The branch of AI focused on giving computers the ability to understand text and spoken words.

  • Tokenization: Breaking text into manageable pieces (words/subwords).
  • Embeddings: Mapping words to mathematical vectors in high-dimensional space so the machine understands context (e.g., "King" - "Man" + "Woman" = "Queen").
  • Modern NLP is powered by Transformers (the architecture behind ChatGPT).

Ethical AI & Limitations

AI is powerful, but not infallible.

  • Data Bias: An AI is only as objective as the data it is trained on.
  • The "Black Box" Problem: Deep neural networks are highly complex; understanding exactly *why* they made a specific decision is often difficult.
  • Computational Cost: Training large models requires massive energy consumption and high-end GPUs.
